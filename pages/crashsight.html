Crash Sight is audio based game for android devices created using App Inventor 2. The game is a based around survival, where the player must avoid enemies and reach a goal to win. You’re a space cadet who has just survived a crash landing on an alien planet; unfortunately, the crash has taken your sight. On the upside, H.Q has contacted you and sent you an escape pod for you to leave. The only problem left is to find the escape pod while trying not to get eaten by the monsters that inhabit the planet or walk off a cliff to your death.

The game is played by simply pointing your device in the direction you want to move to, and then touching the screen when you want to move in that direction. Taking advantage of the orientation sensor in your device, you control where you want to move by pointing in the real directions of north, east, west, and south. You can only choose to move in 4 directions, but a voice in the game will indicate what direction you’re pointing in. Even if you’re pointing southwest, the game is programmed to tell you what direction you’re closer you’re pointing toward rather than being perfectly exact. Sounds the creatures in the game make determine if a path is safe to travel toward or not. If you travel toward a deadly monster or cliff you die, but if you travel toward a safe monster, it will simply move away. The H.Q support guy “Eleven” makes contact with you in areas whenever he gets a good connection. Eleven helps by providing information on what monsters are dangerous and which ones are safe, because the differences are not obvious. He also lets you know how you died when you’re sent to the game over screen.

Credit to Jonathan Genz: the voice actor of Eleven, and Michael Hemingway for the creation of the game's logo.
